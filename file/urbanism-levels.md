Typically in North America:&#x20;

### The downtown

* usually living in a tall condo building
* wealthier than mid-town and the suburbs
* main avenues with straight narrow streets
* people here tend to socialize with strangers
* spoiled for choice for bars and restaurants
* entertainment and events happen everywhere&#x20;

### The mid-town

* usually living in a mid-rise apartment
* either richer or poorer than the suburbs, depending on gentrification
* few main roads with many straight narrow streets
* few lawns but more trees than downtown
* people here tend to know their neighbours
* many pubs and restaurants to choose from

### The suburb

* usually living in&#x20;
  * a detached or semi-detached house
  * a cookie-cutter condo or apartment&#x20;
* typically wealthier than mid-town
* within walking distance of neighbours (as that's all there are)
* wide trunk roads that border labyrinths of smaller roads
* more lawns and greenery
* people here tend to not know (or care about) their neighbours
* your home or your neighbour's home is your pub
* you tend to know how to entertain yourself

### The village

* usually living in a detached house or a trailer
* within walking distance of neighbours
* a main street and a few small roads
* people here tend to know everyone
* one friendly pub / restaurant
* you don't expect the world for entertainment

### The rural

* usually living in a house on a large plot of land
* within driving distance of nearest neighbour
* a large trunk road
* go to the nearest village for any human interaction

### The wilderness

* usually living in a self-made shelter
* off the beaten path
* nowhere near anyone else
